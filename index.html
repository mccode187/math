Results

Given any \(n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\) and \(k \in [0,2n]\), we have \(|\{S : (S \subseteq [0,n]) \land (k \not \in S+S)\}| = 2^{|n-k|} \cdot 3^{\lfloor ((n+1)-|n-k|)/2 \rfloor}\).   Nov 17 2021

Given any \(d \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1}\) and \(n=2d^2-d\), we have \(\min \{ k : \exists A \exists B : (|A|=|B|=k) \land (A,B \subseteq [0,n]) \land (A+B = [0,2n]) \} = 2d\).   Dec 13 2021

Given any \(d \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1}\) and \(n=d^2-1\), we have \(\min \{ k : \exists A \exists B : (|A|=|B|=k) \land (A,B \subseteq [0,n]) \land (A+B \supseteq [0,n]) \} = d\).   Dec 13 2021

Given any \(t \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1}\), \(d=2^t\), and \(n=d^2-(t-1)d-2\), we have \(\min \{ k : \exists A \exists B : (|A|=|B|=k) \land (A,B \subseteq [0,n]) \land (A+_{\leq}B \supseteq [0,n]) \} = d\).   Jan 08 2022

Given any \(m \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\) and \(S \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_m\), if \(|S| > \frac{m}{2}\), then \(S + S = \mathbb{Z}_m\).   Oct 27 2023

Given any \(d \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\) and \(k \in \mathbb{Z}_{2d+1}\), we have \(|\{S : (S \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_{2d+1}) \land (k \not \in S+S)\}| = 3^d\).   Oct 28 2023

Given any prime \(p\) and \(k_1, k_2 \in \mathbb{Z}_{p}\) with \(k_1 \not = k_2\), we have \(|\{S : (S \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_{p}) \land (k_1, k_2 \not \in S+S)\}| = F(p)\).   Oct 28 2023   (proved using method of Daniel Monroe)

Given any \(m \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 2}\) and \(k_1, k_2 \in \mathbb{Z}_{m}\) with \(k_1 \not = k_2\) and \(\gcd(k_2 - k_1, m) = 1\), we have \(|\{S : (S \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_{m}) \land (k_1, k_2 \not \in S+S)\}| = F(m)\).   Oct 29 2023   (result given by Aaron Mei)

Given any prime \(p\) and \(a, d \in \mathbb{Z}_{p}\) with \(d \not = 0\), we have \(|\{S : (S \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_{p}) \land (a, a + d \not \in S+S)\}| = F(p)\).   Oct 30 2023   (equivalent to a previous result)

Given any \(m \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 2}\) and \(a, d \in \mathbb{Z}_{m}\) with \(d \not = 0\) and \(\gcd(d, m) = 1\), we have \(|\{S : (S \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_{m}) \land (a, a+d \not \in S+S)\}| = F(m)\).   Oct 30 2023   (equivalent to a previous result)

Notation

\([i,j]\) denotes the integer interval \(\{i,...,j\}\), or \(\{x \in \mathbb{Z} : i \leq x \leq j\}\).
\(A+B\) denotes the sumset of \(A\) and \(B\), or \(\{a+b : (a \in A) \land (b \in B)\}\).
\(A+_{\leq}B\) denotes a partial sumset of \(A\) and \(B\), specifically, \(\{a+b : (a \in A) \land (b \in B) \land (a \leq b)\}\).
\(F\), the sequence of Fibonacci numbers, is given by \(F(j) = F(j-1) + F(j-2)\), with \(F(0) = 0\) and \(F(1) = 1\).
